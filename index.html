About Lalit

Background

I am a Biology major in the Department of Biological Sciences at Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Mohali with interests in microbiology, developmental biology, and interdisciplinary approaches to biological systems. I am particularly interested in embryogenesis and host–microbe interactions, especially the role of microbial signaling in reproductive and developmental processes. My interests lie in integrating molecular and mechanical perspectives to understand how microorganisms influence organismal systems and biological complexity. I am also interested in exploring mathematical and quantitative approaches to biological questions alongside wet-lab molecular techniques. I completed my schooling under Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan, where I was recognized among the top 1.5% of students. I am also a recipient of the INSPIRE Scholarship awarded by the Department of Science and Technology, India. Beyond academics, I enjoy spending time in nature, gardening, reading, listening to music, and working on craft-based creative projects. I am currently exploring opportunities for interdisciplinary research experience and dissertation projects for my fifth-year BS-MS thesis, and I am always open to discussions and collaborations related to biology and quantitative science.
